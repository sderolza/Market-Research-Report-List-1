<p><p>The sodium-ion battery market includes two main types of separators: dry and wet separators. Dry separators are made from porous materials and rely on mechanical processes to create a barrier between electrodes, offering high thermal stability and efficiency in lithium-ion alternatives. Wet separators, on the other hand, are impregnated with electrolyte solutions, enhancing ionic conductivity and facilitating better performance under varied conditions. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring battery efficiency, safety, and longevity in sodium-ion technology.</p></p>
