<p><p>The coir fibres market is primarily divided into two types: brown fibre and white fibre. Brown fibre, derived from mature coconuts, is robust and often used for products like ropes, mats, and brushes. In contrast, white fibre comes from younger coconuts and is finer and softer, making it suitable for applications in upholstery, textiles, and eco-friendly products. The choice between these fibres depends on their intended use, with each type offering distinct characteristics and benefits for various industries.</p></p>
