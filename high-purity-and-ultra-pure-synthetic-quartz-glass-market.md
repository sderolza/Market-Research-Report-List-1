<p><p>The High-Purity and Ultra-Pure Synthetic Quartz Glass market is segmented into two primary types based on production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Plasma Deposition. CVD involves depositing material from a gas phase onto a substrate, resulting in high-purity quartz with minimal impurities, essential for semiconductor and optical applications. Plasma Deposition, on the other hand, utilizes ionized gases to create barriers or coatings, offering enhanced purity and structural integrity. Both methods cater to industries demanding superior quality and performance.</p></p>
