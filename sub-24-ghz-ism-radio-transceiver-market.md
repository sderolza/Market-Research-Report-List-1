<p><p>The Sub-2.4 GHz ISM radio transceiver market includes various modulation types that cater to different communication needs. FSK (Frequency Shift Keying) and GFSK (Gaussian FSK) are used for efficient data transmission. MSK (Minimum Shift Keying) and PSK (Phase Shift Keying) improve signal robustness. ASK (Amplitude Shift Keying) and OOK (On-Off Keying) are simpler but effective for basic applications. GMSK (Gaussian Minimum Shift Keying) balances bandwidth and performance. LoRa (Long Range) is ideal for low-power, wide-area networks, providing long-distance communication with low energy consumption.</p></p>
