<p><p>The bare thermocouple wire market comprises various types, each suited for specific temperature ranges and environments. Type T (Copper-Constantan) is ideal for low temperatures, Type J (Iron-Constantan) suits general applications with moderate temperatures. Type E (Nickel-Chromium-Constantan) is known for its high sensitivity. Type K (Chromel-Alumel) is widely used due to its versatility in high-temperature applications. Type N (Nicrosil-Nisil) offers stability at high temperatures. Type S (Platinum-Rhodium) and Type R (Platinum-Rhodium) excel in high-temperature scenarios, often in laboratories and industries.</p></p>
