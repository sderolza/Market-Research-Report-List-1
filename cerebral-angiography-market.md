<p><strong>Market Segmentation</strong></p>
<p><strong>The Cerebral Angiography Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Magnetic Resonance Imaging</li><li>Computed Tomography</li><li>Ultrasound</li><li>Nuclear Imaging</li><li>X-ray</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cerebral angiography encompasses various imaging modalities for visualizing blood vessels in the brain. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) provides detailed images without radiation, using magnetic fields. Computed Tomography (CT) combines X-ray data for cross-sectional views, offering fast results. Ultrasound employs sound waves for real-time imaging of blood flow. Nuclear Imaging involves radioactive tracers to assess blood supply and vascular conditions. Finally, traditional X-ray techniques capture images of blood vessels, often aiding in diagnosing vascular abnormalities. Each type caters to distinct clinical needs.</p></p>
