<p><ul><li>Polarography Method</li><li>Current Method</li><li>Optical Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dissolved oxygen sensors for the semiconductor market utilize various methods to measure oxygen levels in water. The polarographic method employs electrodes to detect oxygen concentration through a chemical reaction. The current method measures the electric current produced by the oxygen reduction at the electrode surface. The optical method relies on luminescent materials that change their properties based on oxygen levels, offering high precision and rapid response. Each method ensures critical monitoring for semiconductor manufacturing processes, influencing product quality and yield.</p></p>
