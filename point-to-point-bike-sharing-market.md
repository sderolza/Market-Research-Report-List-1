<p><p>Point-to-Point bike sharing consists of two primary market types: docked and dockless bikes. Docked bike systems require users to pick up and return bikes at designated docking stations, enhancing security and organization. In contrast, dockless bike systems allow users to park bikes anywhere within a designated area, offering greater flexibility and convenience. Docked systems often feature fixed pricing, while dockless models may implement dynamic pricing based on demand, encouraging a more spontaneous use pattern. Both aim to promote eco-friendly transportation.</p></p>
